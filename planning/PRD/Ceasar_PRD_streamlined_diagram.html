SoundPulse

From spotting a trend to collecting royalties — the end-to-end song lifecycle

A fully autonomous record label. One human (the CEO) approves a few critical decisions a week. Software does the rest. This page shows what happens to a single song from the moment an opportunity is spotted to the moment royalties land in the per-song ledger.

Discovery Decision Production Release Growth Earnings
🔍 PHASE 1 Discovery — what should we make?

Watch the streaming charts 24/7, spot what's growing fastest, figure out why, and write a recipe for a song that fits the moment.

Live data
Streaming feeds
Chartmetric (paid charts), Spotify catalog, Tunebat (audio features), MusicBrainz, Genius lyrics, Kworb & radio.
Detector
Breakout Engine
Flags any track growing more than 2× its genre's median. Currently spots ~1,000 breakouts per sweep.
Estimator
Opportunity Quantifier
For each opportunity, estimates dollar revenue (low/median/high) plus a confidence score.
Writer
Smart Prompt
Turns the data into a song recipe: BPM, key, mood, themes, voice type, length, listener target — plus this week's slang and pop-culture references.
↓
✅ PHASE 2 Decision — who sings it?

Existing artists in the roster get scored. If a strong fit exists, the song goes to them. Otherwise, three new persona alternatives are generated and the CEO picks.

Scorer
Assignment Engine
Scores every existing artist across 10 dimensions: genre match, voice fit, lyrical fit, audience overlap, release cadence, momentum, brand stretch, cannibalization risk, diversification, and visual-brand fit.
Threshold
0.68 reuse bar
If the best fit scores ≥ 0.68 → reuse. Otherwise, the LLM persona blender produces three new artist alternatives (A, B, C).
Human gate
CEO via Telegram
CEO sees the proposal, scoring breakdown, and (if create-new) the three persona alternatives. Approves, rejects, or modifies. Quiet hours respected.
CEO approval gate
↓
🎵 PHASE 3 Production — make the song

Build the final prompt around the artist's voice, inject the per-genre song structure, send to Suno, run quality checks. Regenerate up to 3 times if QA fails.

Assembler
Song Orchestrator
Combines artist DNA (voice timbre, accent, ad-libs, prior-song reference) + per-genre structure (e.g., K-pop dance break, drill 4-bar intro) + smart-prompt content + production constraints.
Generator
Suno via EvoLink
Returns audio + lyrics in 30–60 seconds. ~$0.11–0.15 per attempt.
Inspector
Audio QA
10 checks: duration, tempo match, key match, energy match, silence, clipping, loudness normalization, lyric intelligibility, vocal prominence, duplication risk vs. catalog.
Failure path
Regenerate ≤ 3×
Up to 3 regeneration attempts; persistent failure escalates to the CEO. Songs that pass move to Release.
↓
🚀 PHASE 4 Release — get on stores + register every right

Distributor publishes to every DSP. The Submissions Agent then registers the song with every collecting body in the right order — distributor first (returns ISRC/UPC), then PROs, then mechanical, then neighboring rights, then UGC.

Distributor
LabelGrid
Publishes to Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music, Amazon, Tidal, & ~150 other stores. Returns ISRC + UPC identifiers.
Orchestrator
Submissions Agent
Walks the dependency graph in order: distributor → identifier ingestion → PRO lanes → mechanical → neighboring → UGC → sync. Logs every attempt with status + external ID.
Performance rights
ASCAP & BMI
Browser-automated submission via the Fonzworth service. Returns ISWC. Setup is per-label, not per-song.
Mechanical rights
MLC via DDEX
Real API path — XML-formatted submission, no browser needed. Free for members.
Neighboring rights
SoundExchange
Royalties for terrestrial and satellite radio plays. Browser-automated.
UGC monetization
YouTube Content ID
Partnership-gated. Until partnership lands, songs upload to the artist's own YouTube channel via the public Data API.
↓
📈 PHASE 5 Growth — six marketing phases, fourteen agents

Marketing is metric-gated, not time-based. A song moves between phases when it hits the threshold the system requires. Paid spend is only unlocked after an organic winner is identified.

M0 → M1
Asset readiness → Identity seeding
Cover art, hook clips, smart links. Then 1–2 daily posts establishing the artist as a real person. Gate to M2: 10 posts, 15%+ watch-through, 100 followers or 1K organic views.
M2 → M3
Song anchoring → Early amplification
Hook clips, lyric overlays, indie curator pitching. Then micro-influencer seeding + small paid spend on the best clip. Gate to M4: 25K clip views, or 20 creator videos, or 5K streams in 30 days, or 15 playlist adds.
M4 → M5
Breakout acceleration → Catalog compounding
Concentrate budget on winning creatives, push UGC prompts, editorial pitch. Then leverage the win to set up the next single. Gate to M5: 100K+ views, or 50+ creator uses, or 25K+ streams/month, or algorithmic playlist pickup.
↓
💰 PHASE 6 Earnings — collect, learn, reinvest

Royalties flow back from every collector into a single per-song ledger. Outcomes feed the prediction model. Profit gets reinvested into the next cycle.

Inflows
Royalty events
From the distributor, MLC, ASCAP, BMI, SoundExchange, YouTube. Streaming, mechanical, performance, sync, and Content ID royalties all reconciled.
Ledger
Per-song reconciliation
Every payout is matched to the song that earned it, with currency normalization and dedup across overlapping reports.
Outcome
30-day resolution
Each predicted breakout gets a hit / moderate / fizzle label at 30 days post-detection. This is what the ML hit predictor trains on.
Reinvestment
40 / 30 / 20 / 10
Profit allocated: 40% generation, 30% marketing, 20% infrastructure, 10% reserve. Funds the next cycle.

Two feedback loops that compound over time

  • 🔁
    Outcomes → predictions. Every resolved breakout (hit / moderate / fizzle) feeds the ML hit predictor. Better predictions = better blueprints = better songs. Effective once ~6 weeks of resolved breakouts exist (~late spring 2026).
  • 💵
    Revenue → next cycle. 40% of profit funds the next round of generations, 30% the next round of marketing. The catalog compounds.
